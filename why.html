Different Definitions of Law
The law does not seek to describe what must occur, but rather defines rules that individuals have to abide by.
The nature of Law is not universal. Just like language, it varies with people and age.

Types of Law
  • Constitutional Law

    It defines the role and powers of many political entities and the basic rights of citizens.

  • Common Law

    A type of law which is derived from court rulings. It mainly comes from rulings in the Supreme Court.

  • Criminal Law

    As the name suggests it relates to crime. It is mainly concerned around the punishment of offenders.

  • Administrative Law

    The law that governs the government and other administrative bodies actions. These are set by the President/Prime Minister.

  • Statutory Law

    Refers to laws written by a body of legislature. These laws can originate with national, state or local legislatures.
